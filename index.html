About Shakuntala Devi

Shakuntala Devi (1929-2013) was an Indian mathematician and mental calculator, popularly known as the "Human Computer" for her extraordinary ability to perform complex mathematical calculations in her mind.

Born in Bangalore, India, Shakuntala Devi displayed her exceptional mathematical skills from a young age. She traveled the world, demonstrating her abilities and earning recognition for her talent.

Achievements

Shakuntala Devi's most notable achievement was her ability to perform complex mental calculations with incredible speed and accuracy. Some of her remarkable feats include:

  • In 1980, she correctly multiplied two 13-digit numbers in just 28 seconds, earning her a place in the Guinness Book of World Records.
  • She demonstrated her skills on various television shows and in public performances around the world.
  • Shakuntala Devi authored several books on mathematics and mental calculation techniques.

Inspiration and Legacy

Shakuntala Devi's extraordinary abilities and achievements have inspired generations of mathematicians and students worldwide. Her legacy continues to encourage people to explore the fascinating world of mathematics and mental calculations.

Despite facing challenges as a woman in the field of mathematics during her time, Shakuntala Devi's determination and passion for numbers allowed her to break barriers and earn international recognition.
